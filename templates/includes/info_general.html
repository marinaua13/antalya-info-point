General Information

Ensuring the simultaneous application of national legislation and international law is important in terms of preventing human rights violations and creating a decent system for people. In this context, foreigners staying in our country have various rights and obligations. Within the framework of these rights and obligations, a residence permit forms the legal basis for the stay of foreigners in our country.

According to Law No. 6458 “On Foreigners and International Protection”, a residence permit refers to a document that allows you to stay in Turkey. Issued by the competent authorities, this document gives the right to foreign citizens to live in our country for a certain period of time and in a certain place. Important from the point of view of ensuring and maintaining public order, a residence permit is issued subject to the submission of an application by a foreign person, as well as the provision of all necessary documents related to the type of permit required to obtain this type of permit.

MIGRATION SERVICE INFORMATION CENTER (YIMER 157):

For all questions regarding residence permits for foreigners, detailed information can be obtained within 24 hours by contacting the Information Center for Foreigners at 157 within the country or at +90 312 157 11 22 outside the country. Additional information can be obtained from the telephone 24 hours a day.
